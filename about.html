Health Risks from Unhygienic Water

Consumption of unsafe and unhygienic water leads to several serious health complications, especially in rural and low-income regions where water purification infrastructure is often lacking. Below are some of the most common waterborne diseases and their impacts:

  • Diarrhea: A leading cause of death among children under 5 in India. It results in severe dehydration and weakness.
  • Cholera: A bacterial infection that causes watery diarrhea, dehydration, and in extreme cases, can be fatal.
  • Typhoid Fever: Spread through contaminated water, this disease causes prolonged fever, weakness, and intestinal issues.
  • Hepatitis A & E: Liver infections caused by viruses present in impure water sources.
  • Giardiasis: Caused by parasites, leading to cramps, bloating, and frequent loose motions.
  • Worm Infections: Including tapeworm and roundworm, which affect digestion, immunity, and growth in children.
  • Skin Diseases: Infections and rashes due to direct contact or use of contaminated water for bathing.

At WHATIN, we recognize the urgency of this crisis. Our mission is not just water delivery — it’s public health protection. By ensuring verified, RO-purified water supply and offering a digital complaint and tracking system, we aim to reduce these health risks and build safer, healthier communities.
